Lab Instructions
  • Secure a file folder to one end of a baking pan. The folder will act as a shield to prevent sand from blowing too far.
  • Roll a piece of clay into a pencil shape and place it about about 10 cm from the same end of the pan as the file folder. The roll will act as a base for the sand dune to form.
  • Measure 75 mL of fine sand and 75 mL of coarse sand in a beaker. Stir to mix well. Place the sand mixture near the end of the baking pan opposite the file folder.
  • Place the long end of the straw at a 45Â° angle to the mound of sand. Bend the mouth of the straw up then GENTLY and steadily blow the sand toward the roll of clay.
  • Observe the formation of a sand dune on the clay roll.
