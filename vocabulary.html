VOCABULARY

Vocabulary words and their definitions are important to understanding any scientific topic. Below is a list of vocabulary words and definitions pertinent to understanding Earth's changing surface.

WORD DEFINITION WORD DEFINITION
Weathering The breaking down of rock on the Earth's surface, usually by the force of water, wind, or ice. Sediment Small, solid pieces of rock such as sand, silt, or fossils which can be transported and deposited by water, ice, or wind.
Erosion The movement of weathered particles called sediments by water, wind, or ice. Landform A naturally formed feature on the Earth's surface which has a characteristic shape.
Deposition The process by which materials that have been eroded are dropped in a new place. Sedimentary Rock Rock made up of layers of organic and inorganic sediment.
